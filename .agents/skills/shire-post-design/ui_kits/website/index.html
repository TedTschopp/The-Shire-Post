section i · welcome

The counter is open on Tuesdays and Fridays.

The Shire Post is a quiet correspondence house: a place where letters are franked, parcels are held for collection, and the day's notices are pinned beside the door. Most of what we do is for the addressee's eyes only. These pages are the front of the office — what a visitor would see on stepping inside.

section ii · notices to correspondents

Pinned by the door

notice no. 042 · posted 12 march by the clerk on duty

Held for collection

A small number of parcels remain in pigeonhole 14 and may be claimed by their addressees on production of the proper mark. Items unclaimed by Lady-day will be returned to sender.

notice no. 041 · posted 10 march overseas dispatch

A pause in foreign parcels

The Post does not at present accept overseas parcels exceeding two pounds in weight. Letters of any weight continue to be franked as usual. We regret the inconvenience and expect to resume the full service after the spring.

notice no. 040 · posted 03 march private correspondence

A private door

Beyond this counter lies the working office. Correspondents with standing arrangements may apply at the side window on production of their mark and private word. The Post does not at present accept new standing arrangements.
